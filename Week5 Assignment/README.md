Week5 Assignment

# The business problem
TableTurn Restaurant wants to better understand customer reservation behavior in order to reduce no-shows, improve customer engagement, and increase revenue.


# The dataset
The dataset contains 360 restaurant reservation records, including:
- Party Size
- Lead Time Days
- Prior Reservations
- Past No-Show Count
- Average Spend Per Visit
- Distance From Restaurant
- Reservation Value Score


# The clustering method
K-Means Clustering was used after scaling numerical features with StandardScaler.
The Elbow Method was applied to determine the optimal number of clusters.


# The main results
Three customer segments were identified:
1. High-Value Loyal Diners
2. Occasional Low-Value Diners
3. High No-Show Risk Customers


# The business recommendation
The restaurant should reward high-value customers, re-engage lower-value customers, and implement reminder strategies for customers with a history of no-shows.
o	Limitation
K-Means clustering may not perfectly capture all customer behaviors because real-world customer patterns are often more complex than simple cluster boundaries.
